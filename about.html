About This Site

Some people think that the quality of all books cannot be perfectly quantified, so an attempt should not even be made.

These people will probably not enjoy thebestbooksofalltime.com very much.

It's okay, we probably wouldn't have liked them very much either.

So who is this site for?

This site is for the dreamers, the numerical fantasists, the ebullient quantifiers, the dangerously rigorous thinkers with a healthy dollop of IDGAF, and most importantly, the ones who ask SEO goldmine questions like:

  • How do I determine what is literally the best book ever?
  • So what if The Catcher in the Rye is a "classic", where can I find out whether it is actually any good?
  • Where can I find an authoritative book ratings list that is willing to take a firm stand on the Bayesian "c" parameter?

Our site works on a simple idea. We take a seed set of books, find their quantitative ratings -- without treating anyone's rating as intrinsically more valuable as anyone else's, no matter what the USNWR ranking of their pre-school was -- adjust those ratings using some Bayesian secret sauce, and present them for your browsing pleasure.

We're constantly adding new categories with new sets of books that actually make sense -- e.g., you will find science books in the science category, not books about popular culture with a mere tangential link to science.

Want to learn more about us? Read about updates to our site and cool books on our blog.

Is the very idea of this site anathema to you? Please contact us and we'll gladly post your hate mail on our blog.

Thanks for reading!

Disclaimer: This website uses data from Goodreads and ISBNDb. It also has links to Amazon affiliates, in hopes to pay back hosting fees and such.
